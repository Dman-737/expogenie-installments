Installment Payment Scenarios (I-Series)

Installment Payments allow an exhibitor to split their final invoice total into multiple payments. The discount and fee architecture established in EG-FR-2026-001 is unaffected — installments operate as a final step after all pricing rules have resolved. The calculation order for any order with installments active is:

(1) Sum products  →  (2) Apply discount  →  (3) Calculate fee on post-discount total  →  (4) Determine final total  →  (5) Split into installments

Installment Payments and Payment Method Discounts are mutually exclusive — they cannot be active on the same event simultaneously. Payment Method Fees (e.g. credit card processing) are compatible with installments and appear as a line item before the total is split.

New Open Decisions

The following open decisions are added to the parent document's OD tracker and require resolution before e2e development on Installment Payments begins.

OD-05 Derek + Dominica to Confirm

Rounding on Uneven Installment Splits

When a total does not divide evenly across installments (e.g. $1,133.00 ÷ 2 = $566.50 — this case is clean, but $1,100.00 ÷ 3 = $366.666...), the system must decide how to handle the fractional cent. Two options: (A) Round each installment down and add the remainder to the final payment (e.g. 3 × $366.66 + $0.02 added to last = $366.68 final). (B) Round all installments to the nearest cent and allow the final payment to absorb any difference. Option A is the most common practice and is recommended — it ensures the first payment is never inflated. Dominica to confirm QB treatment of the minor variance on the last payment.

OD-06 Derek + E2E to Confirm

Installment Payment Method Constraint

The admin can configure which payment methods are available for installment payments (Credit Card, Invoice/Pay Later, or both). If the exhibitor selects Credit Card for installments, does the CC processing fee apply to each individual installment payment as it is charged, or to the full order total once at checkout? These produce different amounts when the fee is percentage-based. Recommended approach: calculate the fee on the full order total at checkout (as shown in I3 and I4), apply it as a single line item, and include it in the split. This avoids re-calculating the fee on each future payment and keeps the invoice clean. E2E to confirm feasibility with the payment gateway integration.
